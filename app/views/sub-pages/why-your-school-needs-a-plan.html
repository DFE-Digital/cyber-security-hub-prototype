This section provides business justifications for meeting good cyber security incident management.

Key missions a cyber response plan supports

{% call blueContentBox() %}

1. Safeguarding of learners and staff

A response plan supports safeguarding by:

  • enabling early identification and swift response to safeguarding-related threats
  • ensuring that system supporting safeguarding remain available during system outages or attacks
{% endcall %} {% call blueContentBox() %}

2. Continuity of teaching and learning

A response plan supports safeguarding by:

  • allowing proactive identification of threats that could interrupt classes or assessments
  • ensuring recovery plans are in place if digital learning systems are compromised
{% endcall %} {% call blueContentBox() %}

3. Compliance with legal, contractual and regulatory duties

A response plan supports various duties by:

  • having control of access, modification or deletion of sensitive information
  • ensuring compliance with requirements to maintain services and recover from incidents
  • enabling compliance with conditions for insurance schemes or Risk Protection Arrangement (RPA)
{% endcall %} {% call blueContentBox() %}

4. Efficient, trustworthy education and administrative operations

A response plan supports operations by:

  • ensuring schools remain functional despite digital disruption
{% endcall %} {% call blueContentBox() %}

5. Public trust and reputation

A response plan supports reputation by:

  • preventing incidents where possible and responding effectively when incidents occur
  • reassuring legal guardians, staff and external bodies that the school is resilient to attack or system failure
{% endcall %}

Key mission definitions

Safeguarding of learners and staff
Duty to protect the physical, emotional, and digital safety of pupils and employees.

Continuity of teaching and learning
Ensure learning has minimal interruption due to IT issues or cyber incidents.

Compliance with legal, contractual and regulatory duties
Meet obligations under UK GDPR, DPA 2018, Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE), Prevent Duty and Ofsted frameworks.

Efficient, trustworthy education and administrative operations
Maintain confidence in administrative and financial systems, for example payroll, Management Information Systems and parent communications.

Public trust and reputation
Uphold the setting's reputation in the local and wider community.

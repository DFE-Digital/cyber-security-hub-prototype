The Cyber Security Standards are part of the DFE’s Digital and Technology Standards and set clear expectations for cyber security in education settings.

Why the standards are important

Cyber security is the safeguarding of systems, networks, and data from cyber attacks. The methods of safeguarding range from advanced technologies such as firewalls and antivirus software, to basic yet essential practices such as backing up data and using strong passwords.

Meeting these standards shows your commitment to delivering resilient education services by reducing the risk of cyber attacks.

Cyber attacks can lead to:

  • pupil and staff data loss
  • costly budgets from the unexpected costs of responding to a cyber attack
  • financial loss
  • identity theft

What the standards cover

Unlike the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) Cyber Essentials, the DfE Cyber Security Standards are specifically for schools and colleges. The standards address the core principles of cyber governance, processes and strategy and provide guidance on:

  • conducting a cyber risk assessment annually and reviewing every term
  • creating and implementing a cyber awareness plan for students and staff
  • securing digital technology and data with anti-malware and a firewall
  • controlling and securing user accounts and access privileges
  • licensing digital technology and keeping it up to date
  • developing and implementing a data backup plan and reviewing annually
  • reporting cyber attacks

Simple actions to help you meet the standards

These 4 actions don’t require a large budget or IT staff, yet can help prevent data loss, unexpected costs of responding to a cyber attack, financial loss and identity theft:

  1. Implementing multi-factor authentication
  2. Awareness of threats such as ransomware and phishing
  3. Ensuring password security
  4. Backing up data

While these actions can't guarantee complete prevention of an attack, they will reduce the likelihood, lessen the impact, and make incidents easier to manage if they do occur.

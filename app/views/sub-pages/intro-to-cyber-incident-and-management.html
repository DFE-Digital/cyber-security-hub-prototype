Being prepared for a cyber incident helps education settings strengthen the resilience of their services. Implementing a Cyber Response Plan (CRP) can assist schools in achieving the necessary level of readiness to effectively respond to incidents. 

The CRP is designed to be used by the organisation’s Senior Leadership Team (SLT) Digital Leader, working in collaboration with internal or external IT support and any other stakeholders who may be required to take specific actions or make key decisions during an incident.  

Having a Cyber Response Plan in place can:

  • provide insight and examples of actions to be considered when preparing to detect and respond to an incident 
  • help conduct post-incident activities such as maintaining operational resilience and lessons learnt
  • assist in identifying any adjustments needed to the cyber risk assessment

Prepare

  • Understanding the objectives of cyber security incident response planning
  • Building a cyber security Incident Management Policy
  • Building a cyber security Incident Response Plan with playbooks as required that cover specific types of incidents

Respond to incidents by carrying out the Cyber Response Plan

  • Detection and Reporting
  • Triage and Analysis
  • Containment
  • Eradication
  • Recovery
  • Notification
  • Post-Incident Review

Continuous improvement

  • Reviewing and testing policies, plans and procedures

It is important to understand that the most effective incident response plan is one that best fits your requirements, organisational context, cyber security experience and security resources. There is a checklist that you can use to ensure every step of the process has been considered, alongside links to organisations and resources that can help you on this journey.

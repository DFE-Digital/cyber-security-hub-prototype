What is a cyber security incident playbook

Playbooks are concise sets of actions that should be taken in the event of a specific type of cyber security incident.

How playbooks can help you

Playbooks are designed to support you during cyber incidents. They are your resource to tailor and maintain so that you can contain, eradicate and recover from a cyber incident.

When should playbooks be used?

Specific incident types often require specific response actions or slight changes to the standard response process.

Using playbooks for specific incidents allows your Cyber Recovery Team to focus effort more effectively on the important task.

Adapting the playbooks to your needs

Every education setting is different; these playbooks must be treated as templates that you need to change to meet your requirements.

For example, your own customised playbook will include specific references to:

  • your Cyber Recovery Team
  • key stakeholders
  • other key individuals required to respond to that specific incident type
  • contact details for the above

Only technical instructions relevant to your IT assets and in the context of your own IT Support provider should be included.

We have created a set of playbooks that cover a range of common incidents: 

  • Ransomware
  • Business Email Compromise

Who should be involved

You should involve key stakeholders in adapting your playbooks, especially those shaping the Cyber Security Incident Response Plan and those relevant to the incident type.  

You should ensure that:

  • anyone who needs to take action as part of the plan are aware
  • responsibilities are understood
  • access to playbooks and supporting documentation is accessible if  the IT system is disabled

The National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) offers several cyber security initiatives that are highly relevant to schools, helping protect them from cyber threats and ensuring a safe digital learning environment.

Protective Domain Name Service (PDNS)

An initiative designed to protect public sector organisations by blocking access to known malicious domains and preventing data exfiltration via DNS (domain name system) requests.

Key features include:

  • threat intelligence-based blocking which uses up-to-date threat intelligence to prevent access to harmful websites
  • protection against phishing and malware that blocks domains associated with phishing, malware, and command-and-control (C2) communications
  • visibility and monitoring which provides insights into DNS queries to help detect and mitigate threats
  • resilience and performance that ensures high availability and low latency for DNS lookups

How it works

Devices in a protected organisation send DNS queries to the PDNS service instead of using standard DNS resolvers.

PDNS checks the requested domain against its threat intelligence database. If the domain is safe, the request proceeds as usual. If the domain is flagged as malicious the request is blocked, preventing access.

Benefits of PDNS include:

  • a reduction in cyber risks by stopping access to malicious domains
  • minimisation of cyber attack impact, such as ransomware and phishing
  • real-time intelligence to improve cyber security posture
  • being part of the UK government's broader cyber security strategy, which helps public sector organisations enhance their resilience against cyber threats

PDNS designed for schools

The initiative has a specialised version of the service tailored to protect educational institutions from online threats.

PDNS supports schools by:

  • blocking harmful content such as preventing access to phishing sites, malware, and other cyber threats that could compromise school networks
  • supporting safeguarding policies which helps schools comply with Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSiE) guidelines by restricting access to inappropriate and harmful content
  • reducing cyber risks by protecting against ransomware attacks and data breaches that could disrupt learning
  • minimising IT overhead which requires no additional hardware and integrates seamlessly with existing school networks

Implementation

Schools can apply for PDNS through their local authority, multi-academy trust (MAT), or directly via the NCSC.

Once implemented, all DNS requests from school devices are filtered through the PDNS service, ensuring a secure and compliant online environment for students and staff.

Cyber Security for Schools Hub

A dedicated guidance hub providing resources for schools, including advice on security policies, risk management and staff training.

It covers key areas such as:

  • common cyber threats such as phishing, ransomware and data breaches
  • how to secure school networks and devices
  • incident response planning

NCSC Cyber Security for Schools

CyberFirst: Free cyber training for school staff

A program that promotes cyber skills among students and encourages careers in cyber security. While it doesn’t directly impact schools' security, it helps to build cyber awareness among students.

It includes:

  • online and in-person courses for students aged 11 to 17, covering coding, ethical hacking and online safety
  • the opportunity to become a recognised CyberFirst schools which will grant access to cyber security resources

CyberFirst Schools

Early Warning Service

A free alerting service that notifies schools of potential cyber threats such as:

  • compromised credentials found in data breaches
  • school network vulnerabilities being targeted by attackers
  • indicators of ransomware or malware infections

NCSC Early Warning

Exercise in a Box for schools

An interactive tool that helps schools test their cyber security resilience through real-world attack scenarios.

It includes exercises on:

  • responding to a ransomware attack
  • handling phishing emails targeted at staff
  • testing incident response plans

A quick, step-by-step guide for schools to assess and improve their cyber security posture.

It covers:

  • password policies
  • secure backups
  • software updates
  • incident response planning
  • the check your cyber security tool

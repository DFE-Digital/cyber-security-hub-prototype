Cyber security isn’t just a job for IT, it’s everyone’s responsibility to keep  your school safe. 

Effective cyber security in your school consists of both collective and role-specific responsibilities.  

If every staff member follows cyber security best practices, the likelihood of a cyber incident is drastically reduced. In the event an incident does occur, there are clearly defined actions assigned to specific job roles.  

Cyber security tasks for all staff

Every member of staff within your school should understand and adhere to the key cyber security practices, including:

  • recognising common threats, such as phishing emails and ransomware
  • creating strong, unique passwords
  • only using approved software and services
  • following any cyber security policies in place
  • promptly raising any concerns

Role-specific responsibilities in the event of an incident

Understanding the responsibilities of others is crucial to enhancing your school’s communication and the ability to react quickly in the event of a cyber attack.

Every school is different, therefore staffing and structure can vary. This example outlines the individuals who may take part in responding to an attack and tasks they are likely to carry out.

The cyber security terms incident management and incident response are often used interchangeably, however there are significant differences between the two.

{{ greyInfoCard( "/public/images/management-icon.png", "Management icon", "Incident management: how an organisation handles and manages a cyber incident to achieve the best possible outcome." ) }} {{ greyInfoCard( "/public/images/response-icon.png", "Management icon", "Incident response: the specific actions taken to analyse, contain, eradicate and recover from an incident." ) }}

Incident management

Incident management is owned and managed by the Senior Leadership Team (SLT) Digital Lead. Alongside managing any incident response actions carried out by others, incident management includes:

  • all communications
  • any media handling
  • escalations
  • reporting issues

The pulling of the whole response together in a coherent and holistic way is part of incident management.

Incident response 

Incident response is coordinated by the SLT Digital Leader, who assigns response actions to various individuals depending on the size and nature of their school. This includes:  

  • triage
  • in-depth analysis
  • technical recovery actions

Incident response tasks may be assigned to:

  • Technical IT Staff or Managed Service Providers
  • Data Protection Officers
  • Designated Safeguarding Leads
  • Headteachers or other SLT staff
  • Communications or PR Leads
  • Facilities or Site Managers
  • Teaching Staff
  • Legal Advisors

The types of tasks and a recommended process to follow are detailed in the Cyber Security Incident Management and Response Framework.

Managing incidents with fully outsourced IT

Where IT services are wholly delegated to a third-party provider, much or all the technical execution of the Cyber Response Plan will fall under that provider’s contractual obligations.  

The educational setting will retain responsibility for:

  • defining strategic direction and oversight through the Cyber Security Incident Management Policy  
  • creating and maintain the setting’s Cyber Response Plan with clear links and accountability for handover of incident response tasks between the setting and the IT provider
  • ensuring the provider has an appropriate, tested Cyber Response Plan and can demonstrate compliance with the educational setting’s statutory duties, such as safeguarding, UK GDPR and the DfE Cyber Security Standards
  • the SLT, DPO, and safeguarding leads making decisions on regulatory reporting, such as to the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) within 72 hours, alongside staff/parent communications and liaison with governors/trustees 
  • review and assurance, obtaining evidence from the provider of plan testing, incident handling capability and regular updates to contacts and procedures

This will ensure that even where technical control sits externally, the accountability for governance, compliance, and stakeholder communication remains with the educational setting.

This guidance supports both technical and non-technical stakeholders in discussing observations during Triage and Analysis and proposing potential incident types.

Understanding the potential incident type helps to ensure the appropriate people are contacted and may enable the use of a tailored incident response playbook.

This is not a comprehensive list or a detailed technical guide to indicators of compromise. Settings should consult their IT support team and external sources such as the NCSC for more detailed information.

Observation / symptom Possible incident type Supporting indicators
One or more user report receiving emails containing suspicious or unexpected attachments, links to suspicious websites, requests for credentials or demands for money Phishing / social engineering
  • poor spelling or grammar in the email text
  • unexpected or unknown sender
  • urgent requests for action
  • links to fake login pages and/or unusual websites
Multiple users report being locked out or unusual login activity detected Compromised account / unauthorised access, or potentially ransomeware - see below
  • login attempts from unfamiliar locations
  • authentication alerts
  • unscheduled password resets
  • account activity at unusual times
Files are encrypted and a ransome demand appears on user devices Ransomware
  • file extensions changes
  • ReadMe or ransom note files
  • inability to open files
  • sudden system slowness
  • unusually large amounts of data leaving or entering the network
Antivirus or endpoint protection reports malware Malware infection
  • threat quarantined or blocked
  • pop-ups
  • system crashes
  • unusual processes or CPU spikes - visible as sudden system slowness
IT systems and networks suddently become slow, unresponsive or inaccessible, including websites, email and MIS Denial of Service (DoS) or system outage
  • spike in network traffic
  • service crashes
  • repeated service restarts
  • logs show external requests flooding a service
Loss or theft of a laptop, USB stick, or printed files containing personal, financial or otherwise sensitive data Data loss or breach
  • device containing sensitive data unaccounted for or reported lost
  • mis-sent printed materials
  • staff reporting acccidental sharing
  • CCTV or witness account of theft
Student or staff files unexpectedly deleted, moved, or accessed without authorisation Insider threat
  • audit logs showing improper access
  • confessions or alerts from staff or students
  • unusual file changes or deletions
Environmental issue, such as fire, flood or power loss, causes digital systems to become unavailable Environmental disruption
  • known fire or flood event
  • power outage reported
  • system damage after event
Unknown devices or applications appear on the network or in cloud accounts Unauthorised device or shadow IT
  • device not in asset register
  • apps not approved by IT
  • unexpected bandwidth or API usage
Website content altered without authorisation Website defacement
  • public site content changed
  • change not made by admin

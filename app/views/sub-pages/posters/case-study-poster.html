Case study

An academy trust in England with over 6000 pupils and 800 staff experienced an incident in September 2024.

The school’s network supplier identified a large spike in bandwidth. The issue was identified as ransomware. Around 2 million files and 2 terabytes of data were taken, including names, addresses, national insurance numbers and possible bank details. Everything connected to the system was considered infected or compromised. This included catering, costing the Trust £10,000 a day to feed children unable to access digital payment systems.

Learning was significantly impacted, particularly for 1,500 students working towards exams. Lost data included coursework and assignments.

The Trust had a cyber response plan, which they followed immediately. They also had cyber risk cover that gave them access to specialists who helped with the response.

The total estimated cost of damages was £1 million.

Since the incident, the Trust has enhanced their log in security, implemented two-factor authentication and revised their password policy. Attitudes and perceptions have also changed, and they recognise cyber security measures are critical to have in place.

Things you can do now

  • implement multi-factor authentication
  • think about how your back up your information
  • start creating a cyber response plan
  • raise awareness in your school
  • read the Department for Education cyber standards
  • be clear on who is responsible for what when it comes to cyber security in your school

“I strongly believe actions and advise should be focussed on being prepared and being ready to respond to when an attack happens.”

- Educational trust IT and data lead
